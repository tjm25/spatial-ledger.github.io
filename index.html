Every Local Plan makes promises: how many homes will be built, where growth will happen, and which priorities will guide development. But are these promises being kept?

The Spatial Ledger cuts through the complexity. We examine Local Plans across England, scrutinising the details to reveal what they truly commit to, what remains unsaid, and where policies might quietly conflict.

We focus on critical questions:

  • Does the housing target genuinely meet local needs?
  • Is development distributed fairly across towns and villages?
  • Are constraints presented as unchangeable facts or as policy choices?
  • Do the specific site allocations align with the plan's overall vision?

The Spatial Ledger provides clear, evidence-based answers, creating a public record for accountability.

Planning decisions shape our communities profoundly ‚Äì influencing where we can afford to live, the jobs available, and who feels included or left behind.

Yet, these crucial decisions are often buried within lengthy, technical documents. This opacity makes the planning system difficult for most people to engage with ‚Äì until a housing crisis or controversial development makes the consequences unavoidable.

The Spatial Ledger aims to change that. By making local planning promises and policies transparent and understandable, we make the system harder to ignore and easier to hold accountable.

Our process is straightforward:

  • We meticulously read Local Plans and related evidence documents.
  • We identify vague commitments, internal contradictions, and policies that may hinder rather than help sustainable growth.
  • We compare stated ambitions with allocated sites, and targets with likely delivery outcomes.

We then publish our findings clearly, one page per Local Planning Authority, creating a vital public resource ‚Äì free to access, with no spin.

We combine automated analysis using AI tools with careful manual review by planning experts. Our analysis covers adopted Local Plans, sustainability appraisals, strategic housing land availability assessments (SHLAAs), and other core evidence documents.

Our core values are clarity, fairness, and traceability. We strive to present complex information simply, apply consistent standards, and ensure every finding can be traced back to its source. We draw no conclusions without supporting text from the plan documents.

Transparency works both ways: If you believe we've misinterpreted something or made an error, please let us know. We are committed to checking feedback and correcting our analysis where necessary.
